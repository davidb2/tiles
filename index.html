Tiles (Arran, Rodolfo, David, resp.)

While I was working at Microsoft, one of my coworkers gave me and another coworker the following puzzle:
Given an infinite supply of corner trominoes and a single monomino, is it possible to perfectly tile a \(2^n \times 2^n\) grid with these tiles?
Notice that the number of tiles in the grid is \(2^n\cdot 2^n \equiv 4^n \equiv 1\,(\textrm{mod } 3)\), so that is some evidence that this might be possible. Interestingly enough, all three of us had different constructive solutions to the problem. Below are each of our strategies, which have all been proven for a general value of \(n\).
